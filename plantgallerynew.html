Corn (1st): Mainly used for livestock feed and as a key ingredient in food products like corn syrup and tortillas. It also serves as a vital source of biofuel and industrial materials.

Soybean (2nd): Primarily used for livestock feed and fuel. Provides maximum protein per hectare and fuels America behind the scenes.

Hay (3rd): Primarily used as animal fodder, especially for livestock such as cattle and horses. It provides essential nutrients and is crucial for feeding animals during the winter months or when fresh pasture is unavailable.

Wheat (4th) Primarily used for flour in bread and other baked goods. It is a staple food crop that underpins global diets and economies.

Cotton (5th) Primarily used for textile production, cotton is essential for making clothing and home textiles. It also has applications in cooking oil and industrial products like cottonseed meal.
